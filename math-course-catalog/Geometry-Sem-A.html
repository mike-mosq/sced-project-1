              • Curriculum Contents and Pacing Guide - Geometry Sem. A

              This semester is divided into several units spread over 90 days. The pacing guide provides a general timeline for presenting each unit. It is designed to fit your class schedule and is adjustable. The guide is based on a typical 180-day school year with 90 days per semester.

              Unit 1: Foundations of Geometry

              In this unit, students will learn about the origins of geometry and review some basic geometric concepts from previous math courses. Students will complete mathematical proofs to prove theorems.

              Unit 2: Coordinate Geometry

              In this unit, students will learn properties of line segments, lines, and angles and explore relationships among them. Students will create geometric constructions of plane figures using basic tools and technology and find perimeter and area of polygons.

              Unit 3: Transformations and Congruence

              In this unit, students will study properties of triangles and perform rigid and non-rigid transformations of two-dimensional figures, using transformations to establish congruence of triangles.

              Unit 4: Transformations and Similarity

              In this unit, students will study properties of dilations and use non-rigid transformations of two-dimensional figures to establish similarity of triangles. Students will use rigid transformations to explore and establish symmetry of plane figures.

              Unit 5: Polygons

              In this final unit of semester A, students will prove theorems about triangles and parallelograms and will use coordinate geometry to prove geometric theorems algebraically. Students will apply properties of quadrilaterals and angle relationships to solve problems.
