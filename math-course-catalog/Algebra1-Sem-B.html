              • Curriculum Contents and Pacing Guide Algebra 1-Sem B

               

              This semester is divided into five units spread over 90 days. The pacing guide provides a general timeline for presenting each unit. It is designed to fit your class schedule and is adjustable.

              Unit 1: Polynomials

              Summary

               

              This unit is designed to provide students with the tools they need to solve and model complex problems using polynomials. Using the laws of exponents, they will simplify algebraic expressions, including expressions with radicals and rational exponents.

              Students will rewrite monomial expressions and add, subtract, multiply, and divide polynomials. Finally, students will use the distributive property to rewrite polynomial expressions of degree 2.

              Unit 2: Quadratic Relationships

              Summary

              In this unit, students will examine quadratic relationships in detail. Students will use the graphs of quadratic relationships to identify their key attributes, and then they will determine the effects on the graph of the function f(x) = x2 when f(x) undergoes a variety of transformations. Students will describe the relationship between the linear factors of quadratic expressions and the zeros of their associated quadratic functions. They will identify and use quadratic equations in vertex, standard, and factored form. At the end of the unit, students use quadratic functions to model real-world situations.

              Unit 3: Solving Quadratic Equations

              Summary

              In this unit, students will practice a variety of techniques for solving quadratic equations, including inspection, taking square roots, factoring, completing the square, and applying the quadratic formula. At the end of this unit, students will solve quadratic equations using the method appropriate to the initial form of the equation.

               

              Unit 4: Exponential Relationships

              Summary

              This unit will introduce students to exponential relationships and the ways students can use them to model real-world situations. They will examine the key features of exponential functions and their graphs. Then they will describe transformations of exponential functions and practice writing exponential functions. They will write and graph exponential functions that model growth and decay. Finally, students will use different representations to compare exponential functions.

              Unit 5: Mixed Functions

              Summary

              This unit will help students compare different function types and use them to solve mathematical and real-world problems. Students will graph piecewise-defined functions, including absolute value and step functions, and identify transformations of their graphs. They will solve simple systems consisting of a linear equation and a quadratic equation in two variables algebraically and graphically. They will use various methods to solve linear and exponential equations, observing that a quantity increasing exponentially eventually exceeds a quantity increasing linearly. They will see that sequences are functions whose domain is a subset of the integers. Finally, students will compare and translate representations of linear, exponential, and quadratic functions.
